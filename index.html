Research

My research explores how linguistic knowledge is represented in humans and computational models. Integrating behavioral experiments, neuroimaging, and computational methods, I investigate cross-linguistic speech perception, phonological encoding, and the transformation of acoustic input into abstract representations. Beyond speech processing, I examine how language models encode linguistic structures, using information-theoretic and acquisition-focused approaches. My work bridges human cognition and AI, advancing our understanding of linguistic representation.
